Welcome to the Financial Literacy Adventure

Learn about budgeting, saving, investing, and credit by making decisions in this interactive game!

Scene 1: Budgeting

You receive your first paycheck of $1,000. What will you do with it?

  • Understanding income and expenses: You have $500 in essential expenses, and you have $500 remaining.
  • Types of budgets: You could use the 50/30/20 rule, which suggests 50% of your income should go to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings.
  • Tracking spending: It's important to track your spending so you can adjust if you're overspending in certain categories.

Scene 2: Saving and Investing

You have some extra cash after budgeting. How will you manage it?

  • Importance of saving: It's crucial to have an emergency fund to cover unexpected expenses like medical bills or car repairs.
  • Types of savings accounts: A high-yield savings account offers a better interest rate than a regular savings account, helping your money grow faster.
  • Introduction to investments: Stocks offer potential high returns but come with risk. Bonds are lower risk but typically offer lower returns.

Scene 3: Credit

You applied for a credit card, and it has a $300 limit. What will you do?

  • What is credit?: Credit allows you to borrow money and pay it back later, usually with interest.
  • Types of credit: A credit card is a revolving type of credit where you borrow money up to a limit and pay it back later, while a mortgage is for buying a home.
  • Building and maintaining good credit: To maintain a good credit score, it's essential to make payments on time and keep your credit utilization low (use less than 30% of your available credit).
